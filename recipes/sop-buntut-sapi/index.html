Oxtail Soup (Sop Buntut)

★★★★☆ 4.7 (94 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 60m | Cook: 120m | Total: 180m 👥 Servings: 6 orang 📊 Difficulty: Medium 🍽️ Category: Soups & Stews

Oxtail Soup (Sop Buntut) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Oxtail Soup (Sop Buntut) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 kg buntut sapi potong
  • 2 liter air
  • 3 wortel potong
  • 3 kentang potong
  • 2 batang seledri
  • 2 batang daun bawang
  • 5 siung bawang putih
  • 3 cm jahe
  • 1 sdt merica
  • Garam dan kaldu sapi
  • Bawang goreng untuk taburan

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rebus buntut sapi hingga empuk (bisa pakai presto 45 menit).
  2. Angkat buntut, saring kaldunya.
  3. Haluskan bawang putih, jahe, dan merica.
  4. Tumis bumbu halus hingga harum, masukkan ke kaldu.
  5. Masukkan wortel dan kentang. Masak hingga empuk.
  6. Masukkan buntut, garam, dan kaldu bubuk. Koreksi rasa.
  7. Taburi seledri, daun bawang, dan bawang goreng.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Ikan Bakar Jimbaran (Jimbaran Grilled Fish)
