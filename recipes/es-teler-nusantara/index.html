Es Teler (Indonesian Mixed Fruit Drink)

★★★★☆ 4.6 (92 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 9m | Cook: 17m | Total: 25m 👥 Servings: 4 gelas 📊 Difficulty: Easy 🍽️ Category: Beverages

Es Teler (Indonesian Mixed Fruit Drink) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Es Teler (Indonesian Mixed Fruit Drink) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 2 buah alpukat mentega
  • 150 gr nangka potong
  • 2 buah kelapa muda
  • Es serut
  • Sirup cocopandan
  • Susu kental manis
  • Biji selasih

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Keruk daging alpukat, potong nangka, keruk kelapa muda.
  2. Masukkan semua buah ke gelas.
  3. Tambahkan es serut hingga penuh.
  4. Tuang sirup dan susu kental manis.
  5. Aduk rata, sajikan segera.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Pempek Kapal Selam (Palembang Fish Cakes)
