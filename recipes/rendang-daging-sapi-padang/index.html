Beef Rendang Padang (Slow-Cooked Beef in Coconut Milk)

★★★★☆ 4.9 (98 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 80m | Cook: 160m | Total: 240m 👥 Servings: 8 orang 📊 Difficulty: Hard 🍽️ Category: Main Dishes

Beef Rendang Padang (Slow-Cooked Beef in Coconut Milk) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Beef Rendang Padang (Slow-Cooked Beef in Coconut Milk) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 kg daging sapi has dalam
  • 2 liter santan kental
  • 5 lembar daun jeruk
  • 2 batang serai
  • 2 lembar daun kunyit
  • Bumbu halus: 12 bawang merah, 8 bawang putih, 150 gr cabe merah, 4 cm jahe, 4 cm lengkuas, 3 cm kunyit, 2 sdt ketumbar, 1 sdt jintan
  • Garam dan gula secukupnya

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Tumis bumbu halus hingga matang dan minyak terpisah.
  2. Masukkan daging, aduk hingga berubah warna.
  3. Tuang santan, masak dengan api sedang sambil diaduk.
  4. Kecilkan api, masak 3-4 jam hingga santan menyusut.
  5. Aduk sesekali agar tidak gosong.
  6. Masak hingga rendang berwarna coklat gelap kehitaman.
  7. Sajikan dengan nasi hangat.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Patience is key — slow-cook for at least 3 hours until the coconut milk caramelizes.
  • • Use beef with some marbling like brisket or chuck for tenderness.
  • • Let rendang rest overnight — the flavors deepen significantly.

🔄 Variations

  • Chicken Rendang — substitute beef with chicken
  • Rendang Jengkol — use jengkol beans (traditional variant)
  • Quick Rendang — use a pressure cooker for 45 minutes

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Nasi Goreng (Indonesian Fried Rice)
