Pempek Kapal Selam (Palembang Fish Cakes)

★★★★☆ 4.8 (96 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 34m | Cook: 67m | Total: 100m 👥 Servings: 8 buah 📊 Difficulty: Medium 🍽️ Category: Snacks & Appetizers

Pempek Kapal Selam (Palembang Fish Cakes) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Pempek Kapal Selam (Palembang Fish Cakes) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 600 gr ikan tenggiri giling
  • 400 gr tepung sagu
  • 350 ml air es
  • 3 butir telur
  • Cuko: 400 gr gula aren, 8 bawang putih, 15 cabe rawit, 4 sdm cuka, 600 ml air, 1 sdt garam

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Campur ikan, air es, garam, telur. Uleni.
  2. Masukkan tepung sagu sedikit demi sedikit.
  3. Bentuk kapal selam: pipihkan adonan, isi telur, tutup rapat.
  4. Rebus hingga mengapung. Tiriskan.
  5. Goreng hingga keemasan.
  6. Cuko: rebus semua bahan cuko hingga mendidih, saring.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Es Cendol Dawet (Traditional Iced Dessert)
