Es Cendol Dawet (Traditional Iced Dessert)

★★★★☆ 4.7 (94 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 17m | Cook: 34m | Total: 50m 👥 Servings: 6 gelas 📊 Difficulty: Easy 🍽️ Category: Beverages

Es Cendol Dawet (Traditional Iced Dessert) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Es Cendol Dawet (Traditional Iced Dessert) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 150 gr tepung beras
  • 75 gr tepung hunkwe
  • 800 ml air
  • Pasta pandan
  • Sirup: 300 gr gula aren, 150 ml air, daun pandan
  • Santan: 500 ml santan, garam, daun pandan
  • Es serut

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Campur tepung dan air, masak hingga mengental.
  2. Cetak cendol ke air es.
  3. Rebus gula aren dengan air dan pandan hingga mengental.
  4. Rebus santan dengan garam dan pandan, jangan sampai mendidih.
  5. Sajikan cendol dengan es serut, sirup, dan santan.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Martabak Manis Bangka (Sweet Stuffed Pancake)
