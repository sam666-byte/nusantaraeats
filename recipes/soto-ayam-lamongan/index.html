Soto Ayam Lamongan (Traditional Chicken Soup)

★★★★☆ 4.8 (96 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 34m | Cook: 67m | Total: 100m 👥 Servings: 6 orang 📊 Difficulty: Medium 🍽️ Category: Soups & Stews

Soto Ayam Lamongan (Traditional Chicken Soup) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Soto Ayam Lamongan (Traditional Chicken Soup) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 1 ekor ayam kampung
  • 2 batang serai
  • 5 lembar daun jeruk
  • 2,5 liter air
  • Bumbu halus: 10 bawang merah, 6 bawang putih, 5 cm kunyit, 3 cm jahe, 4 kemiri, 1 sdm ketumbar
  • Pelengkap: soun, telur rebus, kol, seledri, bawang goreng, koya, jeruk nipis

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rebus ayam dengan air, buang busa. Masukkan serai dan daun jeruk.
  2. Tumis bumbu halus hingga harum, masukkan ke rebusan ayam.
  3. Masak hingga ayam empuk. Angkat ayam, goreng sebentar, suwir.
  4. Saring kuah, didihkan kembali. Koreksi rasa.
  5. Sajikan dengan nasi, soun, kol, suwiran ayam, telur rebus, dan kuah.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Sate Ayam Madura (Madura-Style Chicken Satay)
