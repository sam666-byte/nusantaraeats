Sate Lilit Bali

★★★★☆ 4.7 (94 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 20m | Cook: 40m | Total: 60m 👥 Servings: 4 orang 📊 Difficulty: Medium 🍽️ Category: Grilled & Satay

Sate Lilit Bali is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Sate Lilit Bali represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 300 gr daging ayam giling
  • 250 gr tuna giling
  • 6 bawang merah
  • 5 bawang putih
  • 3 cm kencur
  • 2 cm kunyit
  • 2 sdt ketumbar
  • Kelapa parut sangrai
  • Batang serai untuk tusuk

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Campur daging, bumbu halus, kelapa sangrai.
  2. Bentuk pada batang serai.
  3. Panggang di atas bara hingga matang.
  4. Sajikan dengan sambal matah.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Sate Padang

Can I make this dish ahead of time?

Yes! Many Indonesian dishes actually taste better the next day as the flavors continue to develop. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.

This dish pairs beautifully with steamed jasmine rice, kerupuk (Indonesian crackers), and sambal (chili sauce).

The spice level is adjustable. You can reduce or increase the chili according to your preference.
