Mie Goreng Jawa (Javanese Fried Noodles)

★★★★☆ 4.5 (90 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 9m | Cook: 17m | Total: 25m 👥 Servings: 3 orang 📊 Difficulty: Easy 🍽️ Category: Main Dishes

Mie Goreng Jawa (Javanese Fried Noodles) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Mie Goreng Jawa (Javanese Fried Noodles) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 500 gr mie telur basah
  • 3 butir telur
  • 5 siung bawang merah
  • 3 siung bawang putih
  • 3 sdm kecap manis
  • 1 sdt merica
  • 1 sdt garam
  • 1 batang daun bawang
  • Sayur: kol, sawi, wortel
  • Minyak untuk menumis

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Rebus mie sebentar, tiriskan. Beri sedikit minyak.
  2. Haluskan bawang merah dan bawang putih.
  3. Tumis bumbu halus hingga harum. Masukkan telur, orak-arik.
  4. Masukkan sayuran, aduk hingga layu.
  5. Masukkan mie, kecap manis, merica, garam. Aduk rata.
  6. Masukkan daun bawang, aduk sebentar. Sajikan.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Oxtail Soup (Sop Buntut)
