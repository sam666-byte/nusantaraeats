Bakso Malang (Complete Beef Meatball Soup)

★★★★☆ 4.6 (92 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 40m | Cook: 80m | Total: 120m 👥 Servings: 8 orang 📊 Difficulty: Medium 🍽️ Category: Soups & Stews

Bakso Malang (Complete Beef Meatball Soup) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Bakso Malang (Complete Beef Meatball Soup) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 600 gr daging sapi giling
  • 150 gr tepung tapioka
  • 3 putih telur
  • 6 siung bawang putih goreng
  • Es batu
  • Kuah: 3 liter air kaldu sapi, bawang putih goreng, seledri
  • Pelengkap: mi kuning, bihun, sawi, tahu goreng, pangsit, sambal

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Campur daging giling, garam, merica, bawang putih. Uleni.
  2. Tambahkan putih telur dan tepung tapioka, uleni hingga kalis.
  3. Bentuk bola-bola, rebus dalam air mendidih hingga mengapung.
  4. Rendam bakso dalam air es agar kenyal.
  5. Kuah: didihkan kaldu, bumbui, masukkan bakso.
  6. Sajikan dengan mi, bihun, sawi, dan pelengkap lainnya.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Gado-Gado Betawi (Indonesian Salad with Peanut Sauce)
