Gado-Gado Betawi (Indonesian Salad with Peanut Sauce)

★★★★☆ 4.5 (90 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 17m | Cook: 34m | Total: 50m 👥 Servings: 4 orang 📊 Difficulty: Easy 🍽️ Category: Main Dishes

Gado-Gado Betawi (Indonesian Salad with Peanut Sauce) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Gado-Gado Betawi (Indonesian Salad with Peanut Sauce) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 150 gr tauge
  • 150 gr kangkung
  • 2 buah kentang rebus
  • 3 butir telur rebus
  • 2 buah tahu goreng
  • Lontong potong
  • Bumbu kacang: 250 gr kacang tanah, 4 bawang putih, 8 cabe rawit, 3 sdm gula merah, 1 sdt garam, 300 ml air
  • Kerupuk emping

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Siapkan semua sayuran rebus dan bahan di piring.
  2. Haluskan bumbu kacang, tambahkan air hangat hingga kekentalan pas.
  3. Siram bumbu kacang di atas tatanan sayur.
  4. Taburi bawang goreng, sajikan dengan kerupuk emping.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Protein swap — substitute the main protein with chicken, tofu, or tempeh
  • Spice level — adjust chili quantity for mild or extra spicy version
  • Dietary — make it vegan by omitting animal products and using plant-based alternatives

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Soto Ayam Lamongan (Traditional Chicken Soup)
