Sate Ayam Madura (Madura-Style Chicken Satay)

★★★★☆ 4.7 (94 reviews)
⏱ Prep: 25m | Cook: 50m | Total: 75m 👥 Servings: 5 orang 📊 Difficulty: Medium 🍽️ Category: Grilled & Satay

Sate Ayam Madura (Madura-Style Chicken Satay) is a beloved Indonesian dish that brings together the rich flavors of traditional spices and fresh ingredients. This authentic recipe guides you through every step, from selecting the right ingredients to achieving the perfect texture and taste. Whether you're a seasoned cook or trying Indonesian cuisine for the first time, this recipe delivers restaurant-quality results right in your own kitchen.

Sate Ayam Madura (Madura-Style Chicken Satay) represents the heart of Indonesian home cooking — flavorful, aromatic, and made with love. This step-by-step guide ensures perfect results every time, whether you're cooking for family, friends, or just yourself.

🛒 Ingredients

  • 600 gr dada ayam fillet
  • Tusuk sate bambu
  • 4 sdm kecap manis
  • 2 sdm minyak kelapa
  • Bumbu kacang: 200 gr kacang tanah goreng, 4 bawang putih, 8 cabe rawit, 3 sdm gula merah, 1 sdt garam, 250 ml air

👨‍🍳 Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Marinasi ayam dengan kecap dan minyak, diamkan 45 menit.
  2. Tusukkan ayam ke tusuk sate.
  3. Haluskan bahan bumbu kacang, tambahkan air hingga kekentalan pas.
  4. Panggang sate di atas bara api, bolak-balik sambil dioles bumbu.
  5. Sajikan dengan bumbu kacang dan lontong.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • • Use fresh, high-quality ingredients for the best flavor.
  • • Adjust seasoning to your taste — traditional Indonesian cooking is all about balance.
  • • Prep all ingredients before you start cooking (mise en place) for a smooth workflow.

🔄 Variations

  • Sate Kambing — substitute chicken with lamb
  • Sate Lilit — Balinese style minced satay
  • Sate Padang — serve with yellow curry sauce

🍚 What to Serve With

This dish pairs perfectly with:

  • Steamed jasmine rice or coconut rice (nasi uduk)
  • Kerupuk (Indonesian shrimp crackers) for crunch
  • Sambal terasi or sambal matah for extra heat
  • Fresh cucumber and tomato slices as a refreshing side
  • Try serving with Beef Rendang Padang (Slow-Cooked Beef in Coconut Milk)
